Failed ATM Heist

According to Fox News, suspects failed to steal an ATM from North Highlands, California at a Safe Credit Union with a forklift. The suspects struggled to knock down the ATM with a forklift but were eventually able to load it up onto a pickup truck, but then the ATM fell out of the back onto the middle of the road. The forklift, recovered at a Lowe's, was seen on traffic cameras being driven through Auburn streets, followed by a silver sedan. The brazen theft has raised concerns about increasing construction site burglaries and challenges in insurance claims, according to a report by KMOV.

Summary
On the early morning of February 5, Auburn police responded to reports of a burglary at a construction site located at 121 49th St. N.W. A forklift had been stolen overnight and used in an attempt to break into a nearby ATM. Despite the brazen effort, the suspects left empty-handed.
Details
  • Location: Auburn, Washington
  • Date: February 5, 2024
  • Incident: Forklift stolen from construction site
  • Outcome: Attempted ATM heist failed; no cash was stolen

Traffic camera footage captured the forklift being driven through Auburn in the middle of the night, accompanied by a silver sedan. The forklift was later recovered at a Lowe's store, and the owner was notified.

Statements

"That’s the first time I’ve had one stolen and used in a crime," said Kyle Fry, president of Northway Construction. While theft is common at construction sites, Fry notes that incidents have increased significantly over the past five years. He adds that insurance claims are rarely filed due to the risk of losing coverage.
